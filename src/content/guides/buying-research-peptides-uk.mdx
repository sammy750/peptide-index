Choosing a research-peptide supplier in the UK comes down to one question: can you trust what is in the vial? The checklist below is what separates a rigorous supplier from a risky one.

> **Research use only.** Everything below concerns sourcing materials for in-vitro laboratory research. It does not concern, and must not be read as guidance on, human or animal use.

## The checklist

1. **A batch-specific Certificate of Analysis.** Purity (HPLC) and identity (mass spectrometry) for the exact batch you receive. See [what is a COA?](/guides/what-is-a-certificate-of-analysis/).
2. **Stated purity (99%+).** A clear HPLC figure, not a vague "high purity" claim.
3. **Cold-chain handling.** Peptides labelled with proper storage conditions and dispatched to preserve stability — see [storage](/guides/how-to-store-research-peptides/).
4. **UK basis and fast dispatch.** A UK supplier means domestic shipping, no customs delays, and GBP pricing.
5. **Clear research-use framing.** A legitimate supplier states products are for in-vitro laboratory research only and does not advise on human or animal use.
6. **Transparency.** Real company details, contactable support, and documentation available on request.
